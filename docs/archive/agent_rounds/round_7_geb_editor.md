# Round 7 GEB Editor Memo

Role: Editor. Personality: strict journal editor focused on clarity, proof standards, venue fit, and reader expectations.

## Verdict

The Round 7 GEB draft is now on the right track for Games and Economic Behavior. It is framed as a finite-game paper about fast preference closure, not as a broad essay about social media, AI, or macro trends. The separation among theorem, example, simulation, and working-paper material is substantially cleaner than before.

My verdict is **revise before submission, but no longer conceptually misfiled**. The GEB version has a defensible theorem spine: fixed-\(E\), fixed-\(\ell\) closure; reduced subjective games; mixed best-response invariance; non-invariance test; selection degeneracy within closure-equivalence classes; law-level selection as a separate object; and proxy/material alignment. The working-paper version can remain broader, but it should not be treated as the GEB submission.

## Blocking Issues

1. **Proof standards are still too compressed for journal submission.** Appendix A is directionally correct, but Lemma 1 should state the convergence object more precisely: fast time, fixed slow state, basin/global attraction, and the sense in which the reduced game is obtained in the \(T_\theta\to 0\) limit. GEB referees will not require a full singular-perturbation treatise, but they will expect the limit claim to be mathematically crisp.

2. **The q-indexed notation in Theorem 1 needs one more pass.** The draft introduces \(\Gamma_\ell^\star(E;q)\) in the selection-rule definition after earlier defining \(\Gamma_\ell^\star(E)\). This is the right idea, but the notation should be introduced explicitly before it is used: before closure, \(q\) may induce a post-closure game; under unique closure the \(q\)-dependence collapses.

3. **Example 1 needs one feasibility sentence.** The example cleanly shows a Nash-set change, but a skeptical reader may ask whether the reversed subjective table is actually generated by an admissible closure law. Add a sentence saying the example assumes the closure-law family contains a law whose attracting branch induces the displayed subjective payoffs, or explicitly define a trivial \(H\) with that fixed point.

4. **The simulation section needs reproducibility discipline.** It now correctly says the audit is illustrative rather than evidence for the theorems. Still, a submission version should report the seed or replication entry point, the random payoff distribution, the selection convention, the shift threshold, and Monte Carlo standard errors or confidence intervals for the reported rates.

5. **The exact/noisy alignment distinction must be impossible to miss.** The current wording is acceptable, but the table label "Noisy proxy aligned with material welfare" should be paired with a short note directly under Table 1B: Proposition 3 gives zero loss only under exact maximizer-set alignment; Table 1B uses noisy approximate alignment.

## Recommended Final Writer Edits

1. Add a short "Simulation Design" paragraph after the first paragraph of Section 8 with \(N=5{,}000\), random draw distribution, threshold, equilibrium-selection convention, seed/script path, and standard errors.

2. Add one sentence before Theorem 1 defining \(\Gamma_\ell^\star(E;q)\), then state that under unique closure it equals \(\Gamma_\ell^\star(E)\) for all \(q\in Q_\ell\).

3. Add the feasibility sentence to Example 1 so the example is visibly an admissible fast-closure example rather than an arbitrary payoff replacement.

4. Replace "material Nash predictions" in the conclusion with the more exact phrase "Nash sets of the reduced subjective and material benchmark games" or "equilibrium predictions under material payoffs." The current phrase is understandable but slightly loose.

5. Keep the main-text proofs short, but make Appendix A less sketch-like. The body should state results; the appendix should carry the proof burden.

## Visual QA Targets

1. The abstract: check that \(T_\theta\), fixed-\(E\), fixed-\(\ell\), and \(\theta_\ell^\star(E)\) render cleanly.

2. Section 5: check the expected-payoff and best-response boxes on desktop and mobile widths; these are the most notation-heavy display equations.

3. Proposition 2 and Example 1: verify that theorem boxes do not crowd nested equation boxes and that the displayed dominance equations fit without clipping.

4. Section 8: inspect both figures and both tables. Tables must not overflow awkwardly on narrow screens, and the figure captions should remain legible.

5. Appendix A: inspect headings and proof paragraphs for visual density; the appendix currently risks looking like compressed notes rather than final proof text.

## Working Paper Notes

The working paper should keep the WDI material, but only as an empirical appendix and measurement agenda. It should not imply that WDI identifies preference closure or platform effects.

Before public circulation, the working paper should import the Round 7 GEB theorem refinements: post-closure admissible selection rules, the corrected role of Proposition 2, the explicit Nash-set-change example, and the exact-versus-noisy proxy alignment distinction.

The working paper's status note is helpful and should remain. Its proof-sketch language is acceptable internally, but a circulated working-paper PDF should either mark proofs as sketches consistently or promote them to proper appendix proofs.
