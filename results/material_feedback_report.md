# Material Feedback Results

Generated by `python3 scripts/run_material_feedback_analysis.py`.

## Model

The fast subsystem maps material capacity `K` into the subjective
attractiveness of a substitute behavior:

```text
p(K) = logistic(beta * (q + z - rho K))
```

The slow capacity stock then evolves as:

```text
Kdot = alpha + r K^2 (1 - K) - d K - L p(K)
```

The term `alpha + r K^2(1-K)` represents baseline and practice-based
repair, `dK` is maintenance decay, and `L p(K)` is damage or crowding
out from the substitute behavior. Capacity is projected onto `[0,1]`:
if the unprojected drift points below zero or above one, the boundary
is treated as a feasible boundary state. This is a stylized normal
form, not an empirical estimate.

## Main Finding

In the self-correcting calibration, weak feedback has one stable
high-capacity state. In the capacity-trap calibration, the same model
has two stable states separated by an unstable threshold. In the random audit, weak feedback
is mostly single-state, trap-prone feedback has the highest rate of
low-high traps, and collapse-prone feedback more often reaches the
lower boundary of the projected capacity interval.

## Interpretation

- A fixed-preference model can describe choice once the payoff criterion is
  formed, but it misses whether today's choice changes tomorrow's
  capacity and therefore tomorrow's payoff criterion.
- Nash equilibrium remains the consistency condition inside the induced
  subjective game; it does not certify the feedback loop that produced
  the subjective payoffs.
- Darwinian or selection language becomes operational only after naming the
  capacity stock: solvency, health, fertility capacity, friendship skill,
  learning, trust, institutional competence, or demographic continuity.
- The same formal loop covers harms and repairs. A platform can raise
  `z`, `beta`, or `L`; a therapeutic, educational, or AI-assistant design
  can lower those terms or raise repair.
