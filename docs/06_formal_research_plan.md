# Formal Research Plan

## 1. Core Mathematical Environment

Time is discrete, `t = 0, 1, 2, ...`. There is a population distribution `mu_t` over preference states `theta in Theta`.

An agent with preference state `theta` chooses an action:

```text
a(theta, m) in argmax_a E[u(x(a, a_-i, m), a, theta)]
```

A platform, institution, or social environment chooses a policy:

```text
m in M
```

Preferences evolve by:

```text
theta' ~ K_m(. | theta, a, x)
```

Population shares evolve by selection:

```text
mu_{t+1}(B) =
  integral_Theta K_m(B | theta, a(theta, m), x(theta, m))
  F(theta, a(theta, m), x(theta, m), m) mu_t(d theta)
  / Phi(mu_t, m)
```

where:

```text
Phi(mu_t, m) =
  integral_Theta F(theta, a(theta, m), x(theta, m), m) mu_t(d theta)
```

This nests standard theory when:

```text
K_m(theta | theta, a, x) = 1
```

and there is no independent platform policy.

## 2. Three Welfare Objects

The model should keep three rankings separate:

### Current Subjective Welfare

```text
W^S_t = integral u(x_t(theta), a_t(theta), theta) mu_t(d theta)
```

### Fitness Or Material Welfare

```text
W^F_t = integral F(theta, a_t(theta), x_t(theta), m_t) mu_t(d theta)
```

### Meta-Preference Or Autonomy Welfare

```text
W^M_t = integral V(theta_t, theta_{t+1}, x_t, a_t) mu_t(d theta)
```

The point is not to decide immediately which is morally correct. The first paper should show that these objects can diverge even in extremely small models.

## 3. Equilibrium Concept

An endogenous-preference equilibrium is a tuple:

```text
(sigma, m, mu)
```

where:

- `sigma(theta)` is the action rule,
- `m(mu)` is the platform or institutional policy,
- `mu` is an invariant or path distribution over preference states.

For the stationary case:

```text
mu = T_m(mu)
```

where `T_m` is the replicator-transition operator induced by local best responses and `K_m`.

For platform control, add:

```text
m in argmax_m Pi(m, mu)
```

where `Pi` might be engagement, advertising revenue, ideology, market share, or any other institutional objective.

## 4. Model Hierarchy

### M0: Static Utility Benchmark

Purpose: establish that the framework nests ordinary utility representation.

Deliverable:

- proposition showing equivalence under degenerate `K`;
- note on revealed-preference tests when all observations are drawn from one `theta`.

### M1: Endogenous Taste Drift

Purpose: prove and simulate fitness-utility inversion.

State:

```text
theta in [0, 1]
```

Utility:

```text
u(o, h; theta) = theta log(o) + (1 - theta) log(h)
```

Transition:

```text
theta' = theta + eta[b_m(theta)(1 - theta) - c theta]
```

Fitness:

```text
F(theta) decreasing after some threshold
```

Target proposition:

If `eta * b_m` is large enough relative to the maximum selection gradient against high `theta`, the stationary distribution is concentrated near the platform-preferred attractor even when that attractor has lower fitness.

### M2: Indirect Evolutionary Game With Within-Life Preference Mutation

Purpose: extend Dekel-Ely-Yilankaya style preference evolution by adding a mutation channel controlled by institutions.

Agents play a game `G`. Subjective utility differs from material payoff:

```text
u_i(a_i, a_j; lambda_i) = pi_i(a_i, a_j) + lambda_i pi_j(a_i, a_j)
```

Population selection is by `pi_i`, while `lambda` evolves through `K_m`.

Target proposition:

Stable action outcomes can be reversed by small but persistent preference-transition bias, even when material selection favors the opposite type.

### M3: Platform Preference-Control Game

Purpose: make the AI/social-media actor endogenous.

Platform objective:

```text
Pi(m, mu) = engagement(m, mu) - cost(m)
```

User behavior:

```text
a(theta, m) maximizes u(a, theta)
```

Preference transition:

```text
K_m
```

Target proposition:

If engagement is increasing in predictability or intensity of taste, the platform may choose `m` that reduces preference diversity and shifts population mass toward a narrow taste basin, even if this lowers `W^F`.

### M4: AI-Augmented Cultural Transmission

Purpose: connect to Bisin-Verdier and make timescale explicit.

Child preference:

```text
theta_child =
  alpha theta_parent
  + beta theta_peer
  + gamma theta_algorithm
  + epsilon
```

The comparative static is `gamma` per unit time. A model where `gamma` rises and period length falls can produce cultural drift faster than family or school socialization.

### M5: Revealed Preference Under Preference Drift

Purpose: connect to empirical economics.

Observation:

```text
(p_t, x_t)
```

Standard revealed preference assumes choices are generated by one stable preference relation. We instead test whether data can be rationalized by a bounded-drift sequence:

```text
theta_{t+1} in Gamma(theta_t, exposure_t)
```

Target output:

- define "K-GARP" or "bounded-preference-drift GARP";
- show that ordinary GARP violations can be reclassified into price effects versus preference-transition effects only with extra restrictions.

## 5. Theorem Candidates

### Proposition 1: Static Embedding

If `K` is degenerate at the current preference state and platform policy is absent, endogenous-preference equilibrium reduces to the standard model.

Use: credibility and clean nesting.

### Proposition 2: Preference Laundering

If a mechanism can choose a transition kernel over a sufficiently rich `Theta`, then for many allocations `x` and `y`, it can induce final preferences under which `y` is Pareto-superior to `x`, even when initial preferences rank `x` above `y`.

Use: motivates initial-preference, meta-preference, or constitutional welfare criteria.

### Proposition 3: Timescale Dominance

In one-dimensional taste dynamics with monotone platform drift toward `theta_H` and monotone fitness penalty near `theta_H`, there exists a plasticity threshold above which the stationary distribution concentrates near `theta_H`.

Use: formalizes "AI-speed preference capture can overpower Darwinian selection."

### Proposition 4: Nash Incompleteness

An action profile that is a Nash equilibrium at `theta_t` need not be dynamically stable under `K_m`; stability requires a fixed point of the joint action-preference operator.

Use: bridges Nash equilibrium to endogenous utility.

### Proposition 5: Welfare Trilemma

There exist paths `z` and `z'` such that:

```text
z preferred by initial preferences
z' preferred by final preferences
z and z' ranked differently by fitness
```

No Pareto criterion over allocation paths alone can resolve the ranking.

Use: gives the paper its normative bite.

## 7. Empirical Plan

The first paper can stay theoretical, but the empirical section should name observable implications.

Candidate proxies:

- social-media exposure and time use;
- willingness to pay for deactivation or platform access;
- offline social activity;
- fertility, dating, and household formation;
- cosmetic procedure uptake and body-image investment;
- content feed experiments and recommender-system interventions.

The empirical hypothesis is not "social media causes all preference change." It is:

```text
algorithmic exposure predicts changes in revealed choices
that are too fast or too directionally aligned with platform incentives
to be well-described as slow cultural drift.
```
