# Literature Matrix

This matrix tracks how each strand enters the project. It is a working file, not a final bibliography.

| Strand | Source | Core Object | Key Result Or Use | Project Move |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Utility representation | Debreu, "Representation of a preference ordering by a numerical function" | Preference relation over alternatives | Conditions for utility representation | Replace one relation with a path of local relations indexed by `theta_t`. |
| Revealed preference | Samuelson, Houthakker, Afriat, Varian | Choice data and budget sets | Choice consistency can rationalize a stable utility function | Define bounded-drift revealed preference tests when `theta_t` changes. |
| Stable deep tastes | Stigler and Becker, "De Gustibus Non Est Disputandum" | Stable preferences plus consumption capital | Apparent taste changes can be shadow-price changes | Treat AI-targeted consumption capital as a controllable transition channel. |
| Rational addiction | Becker and Murphy, "A Theory of Rational Addiction" | Consumption stock and dynamic complementarities | Addictive consumption can be modeled as forward-looking maximization | Use stock dynamics but separate subjective utility from fitness and platform objective. |
| Endogenous time preference | Becker and Mulligan, "The Endogenous Determination of Time Preference" | Investment in patience | Time preference can be shaped by costly effort | Generalize investment to platform-induced preference transition. |
| Habit foundations | Rozen, "Foundations of Intrinsic Habit Formation" | Axioms for habit-dependent preferences | Intertemporal representation of habit formation | Use as an axiomatic reference for state-dependent local utility. |
| Cultural transmission | Bisin and Verdier | Population dynamics of cultural traits | Family/socialization choices generate stable heterogeneity or homogeneity | Add algorithmic socialization with a much shorter period length. |
| Institutional preference formation | Bowles, "Endogenous Preferences" | Institutions and preference evolution | Economic institutions shape motives and values | Treat platform policy as an institution with a measurable transition kernel. |
| Two-speed preference dynamics | Sandholm, "Preference Evolution, Two-Speed Dynamics, and Rapid Social Change" | Behavior adjusts quickly while preference distributions evolve slowly | Closest timescale foil for our reversed limit | Keep as benchmark; our fast variable is preference adaptation itself. |
| Nash and preference evolution | Ely and Yilankaya, "Nash Equilibrium and the Evolution of Preferences" | Normal-form games with subjective preferences and fitness payoffs | Fitness payoffs need not equal preferences, but aggregate play links to Nash of material game under their assumptions | Use as finite-game ancestor; fast payoff formation changes the subjective game before material selection. |
| Indirect evolution | Dekel, Ely, and Yilankaya | Subjective preferences, objective fitness | Stable outcomes depend on observability and fitness selection | Add within-life preference mutation controlled by `K_m`. |
| Dynamic evolution | Heifetz, Shannon, and Spiegel | Distribution of preferences in strategic games | Limiting distributions of preferences in broad games | Use as foundation for general dynamic preference distributions. |
| Generic payoff distortions | Heifetz, Shannon, and Spiegel, "What to Maximize if You Must" | Distorted objectives in strategic games | Payoff maximization is not generically justified by evolutionary arguments | Supports separating subjective utility, material payoff, and selected object. |
| Moral preference evolution | Alger and Weibull | Fitness game and moral preferences | Stable moral weight depends on matching assortativity | Add algorithmic matching and exposure as controllable assortativity. |
| Chosen preferences | Bernheim et al., "A Theory of Chosen Preferences" | Worldviews and preference choice | Agents can adopt worldviews shaping experience | Use for endogenous preference choice and flexibility. |
| Adaptive utility | Robson, Whitehead, and Robalino | Real-time adaptation of utility under limited discrimination | Utility can adapt dynamically to reward distributions | Use as microfoundation for fast preference states, not as equilibrium-selection model. |
| Meta-preferences | Hayashi, "Meta-preference, endogenous preference formation and dynamic choice" | Recursive layers of meta-preferences | Preference formation can be modeled with recursive utility over successor preferences | Use as welfare/autonomy layer over `theta -> theta'`. |
| Behavioral welfare | Bernheim and Rangel | Welfare under nonstandard choice | Revealed preference welfare needs refinement with behavioral agents | Extend to preference-changing environments. |
| Social media welfare | Allcott, Braghieri, Eichmeyer, Gentzkow | Facebook deactivation RCT | Deactivation changed online/offline activity, well-being, and valuations | Empirical motivation that revealed valuations may overstate welfare. |
| Feed algorithm experiments | Guess et al. | Algorithmic versus chronological feeds | Feed changes affected online behavior more than downstream political attitudes in the study window | Motivates timescale and outcome-domain distinctions. |
| AI/social-media political economy | Acemoglu, Ozdaglar, Siderius | AI recommendation and digital ads | AI-powered platforms can polarize voters through engagement and ad channels | Use as nearby platform objective model. |
| Engagement optimization | Kleinberg, Mullainathan, and Raghavan | Inconsistent preferences and content manifolds | Engagement can move in directions that lower welfare | Main platform-welfare threat; distinguish by making future utility itself endogenous. |
| Recommender degeneracy | Jiang, Chiappa, Lattimore, Gyorgy, and Kohli | User dynamics plus recommender feedback | Recommender decisions can influence beliefs/preferences and create degeneracy | Motivates proxy feedback and fast adaptation. |
| Algorithmic confounding | Chaney, Stewart, and Engelhardt | Feedback between recommendations and training data | Recommendation feedback can homogenize behavior without utility gains | Supports platform application and alignment/misalignment tests. |
| Recommender feedback loops | Berkeley EECS recommender dynamics thesis | Sequential recommendation with feedback | Recommendations affect behavior and future inferred preferences | Map recommender feedback into `K_m`. |
| AI preference manipulation | Ashton and Franklin | AI/ML preference learning and manipulation | Learning and changing preferences can be hard to distinguish | Use as conceptual support for meta-preference restrictions. |
