# Application Map

This note lists candidate domains where the material-capacity feedback model
may clarify current economic and social puzzles. These are not proof of the
model. Each application is useful only if it names the payoff-forming exposure,
the substitute behavior, the material capacity, and the competitive score.

## Strongest Current Applications

### AI Companions And Loneliness

- Exposure: always-available synthetic interaction, memory, responsiveness,
  low rejection risk.
- Substitute: synthetic companionship instead of attempts at ordinary human
  contact.
- Material capacity: offline contact attempts, friendship repair, social
  anxiety, invitations accepted, durable human ties.
- Competitive score: retention, subscription, clinical improvement, restored
  social life, or institutional adoption.
- Why it fits: the same technology can be a bridge or a sink depending on
  whether it rebuilds social capacity.

### Sports Betting And Solvency

- Exposure: live-betting interface, frictionless deposits, in-game prompts,
  parlays.
- Substitute: repeated micro-wagers as part of fandom.
- Material capacity: savings buffer, credit utilization, missed payments,
  ordinary budget control.
- Competitive score: handle, retention, revenue, or household solvency.
- Why it fits: the local payoff of risk can be coherent while repeated behavior
  changes future financial capacity.

### Political Outrage And Verification Capacity

- Exposure: ranked feeds, news influencers, parasocial trust, emotionally
  fluent formats.
- Substitute: outrage consumption or broadcasting instead of slower
  verification.
- Material capacity: correction acceptance, institutional trust, cross-cutting
  attention, peaceful contestation.
- Competitive score: engagement, audience share, verification capacity, or
  institutional resilience.
- Why it fits: Nash-style escalation can be coherent after attention payoffs
  are formed, while selection can reward formats that degrade verification.

### Food Environments And GLP-1 Repair

- Exposure: hyper-palatable food environments or appetite-changing medicine.
- Substitute: repeated high-reward consumption, or medication-assisted appetite
  change.
- Material capacity: metabolic markers, appetite regulation, durable behavior
  outside the product.
- Competitive score: sales, adherence, health improvement, or product
  dependence.
- Why it fits: GLP-1s provide a positive repair case; the question is whether
  changed appetite rebuilds durable capacity.

### Dating Retreat And Social Capacity

- Exposure: rejection avoidance, dating-app discouragement, pornography,
  gaming, safety fears, reputational risk.
- Substitute: low-risk digital or avoidant interaction.
- Material capacity: courtship skill, mixed social-network size, relationship
  formation, rejection anxiety.
- Competitive score: substitute retention, social-capacity improvement, or
  household formation.
- Why it fits: it is a candidate threshold case, not a settled diagnosis.

## Broader Candidate Domains

- appearance markets and filters;
- youth attention and identity loops;
- housing and delayed adulthood;
- low fertility and the wanted-children gap;
- migration and identity backlash;
- AI labor substitution and skill formation;
- online nationalism and international trust.

These should stay secondary until they are tied to measurable capacities and
identification strategies.

## Empirical Questions

- Does exposure change what feels rewarding?
- Does the changed payoff alter substitute behavior?
- Does substitute behavior change later capacity?
- Does later capacity feed back into payoff formation?
- Which competitive score determines which rule spreads?
- Is the evidence compatible with a fixed-preference account?

## Style Rule

Applications should make the model useful, not topical. Avoid lists of social
problems unless each case advances exposure, substitute, capacity, and score.
