## Main Readout

A material-reality signal does not automatically repair an induced-preference
loop. If the signal is only the current capacity drift, then the roots of
the reduced capacity equation are unchanged: at any steady state the drift
signal is zero. It can damp motion, but it does not eliminate the trap.

In the baseline trap calibration, the maximum numerical root displacement
from drift-signal feedback is `1.192e-10`.

In this diagnostic, the tested current-drift signal does not remove
the low-capacity trap. The displayed stock-channel sweep, here through
the capacity-protection parameter `rho`, can remove it. In the same
calibration, the low-capacity trap disappears once `rho` is about
`3.10` on the grid used here.

Competition adds a Darwinian layer. It does not rewrite an individual
rule's capacity dynamics. It reallocates population share, capital,
attention, or institutional prevalence toward rules that score higher
on the operative competitive score `S`. Absolute material survival is
tracked separately by material growth `g`. With material-viability
competition, `S` is aligned with `g` and the capacity-building rule
wins. With engagement-proxy competition, the capacity-draining rule
can win while material population mass falls.

## Interpretation

- Current pain or deteriorating-flow reports are not enough if
  they vanish once the system settles into a low-capacity state.
- In this diagnostic, automatic correction comes from a persistent
  material state channel, or from competition when the competitive score
  is aligned with material viability.
- This is a Nash/Darwin result rather than advice: the subjective Nash map
  produces behavior, behavior moves capacity, and selection can only favor
  fitter rules if the material evaluator actually governs reproduction,
  survival, exit, imitation, or institutional persistence.
- If competition is over a proxy such as engagement, the Darwinian operator
  is still active, but it selects the rule that wins the proxy contest.
  Relative victory under `S` and material survival under `g` are different
  claims.
