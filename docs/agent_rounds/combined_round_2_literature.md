# Combined Round 2: Literature

Date: 2026-06-23

Role: historically careful, novelty-sensitive, allergic to overclaiming.

Target: `paper/combined_fast_preference_closure_v1.html`

## Main Findings

1. The novelty should be framed narrowly: established social-interaction and cascade machinery is reinterpreted as a fast utility-closure law and then fed into Nash/material-selection analysis.
2. The paper should avoid saying it "reverses" indirect evolutionary preference timing too strongly. Better: it interposes a fast preference-closure stage before play and material selection.
3. The social-feedback equations are close to Brock-Durlauf/logit social interactions, threshold contagion, and Ising mean-field fixed points. The paper should say these mechanisms are deliberately borrowed.
4. The recommender-system connection should remain conditional; the manuscript does not estimate causal effects of any real platform.
5. Related Work needed clearer filing cabinets: stable-preference benchmark, endogenous preferences, indirect evolution, social feedback, and recommender/proxy objectives.

## Integrated Changes

- Split Related Work into named paragraphs.
- Added stable-preference and endogenous-preference context.
- Reworded the novelty claim around fast closure rather than novelty of herding/fashion mathematics.
- Replaced empirical-sounding language such as "can change what people want" with "are modeled as changing what people want."
- Replaced "flips the preference state persistently" with "selects the high stable branch in the model."
