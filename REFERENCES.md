# References and Source Map

This is the active source map for the material-capacity feedback paper. It is a
working bibliography, not a complete citation file. Before submission, verify
every bibliographic detail against the original publication.

## Fixed-Preference Baseline

These sources define the conventional benchmark: utility represents stable
preferences or is recovered from observed choice.

- von Neumann and Morgenstern utility theory: expected utility representation
  under completeness, transitivity, continuity, and independence.
- Samuelson, Houthakker, Afriat, and Varian: revealed preference and utility
  recovery from choice data.
- Hal Varian, "Revealed Preference":
  https://people.ischool.berkeley.edu/~hal/Papers/2005/revpref.pdf

## Endogenous Preferences

These sources establish that preference formation is already a serious
economics topic. The paper should not claim novelty merely from making utility
endogenous.

- Samuel Bowles, "Endogenous Preferences: The Cultural Consequences of Markets
  and Other Economic Institutions," Journal of Economic Literature, 1998.
  RePEc entry:
  https://econpapers.repec.org/RePEc:aea:jeclit:v:36:y:1998:i:1:p:75-111
- Bernheim endogenous-preferences research agenda:
  https://bernheim.people.stanford.edu/research-fields/endogenous-preferences
- Bisin and Verdier cultural transmission models: useful background for
  transmission mechanisms and population dynamics.

## Evolutionary Preferences And Nash

These sources keep Nash and Darwinian selection in the right role. Nash is a
consistency condition after payoffs are specified. Selection requires naming
the object selected and the score being selected for.

- Dekel, Ely, and Yilankaya, "Evolution of Preferences," Review of Economic
  Studies, 2007.
  https://academic.oup.com/restud/article-abstract/74/3/685/1563303
- Ely and Yilankaya, "Nash Equilibrium and the Evolution of Preferences,"
  Journal of Economic Theory, 2001.
  https://ideas.repec.org/a/eee/jetheo/v97y2001i2p255-272.html
- Sandholm, "Preference Evolution, Two-Speed Dynamics, and Rapid Social
  Change," Review of Economic Dynamics, 2001.
  https://ideas.repec.org/a/red/issued/v4y2001i3p637-679.html

  https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/aer.20190390

## Capacity, Addiction, Aspirations, And Lock-In

These are conceptual anchors for the slow material stock. Use them to motivate
why repeated choice can change future feasible payoffs.

- Grossman health-capital models.
- Becker and Murphy rational addiction.
- Koszegi and Rabin reference-dependent preferences.
- Genicot and Ray aspirations.
- Brock and Durlauf social interactions.
- Arthur increasing returns and lock-in.

## Welfare And Normative Boundaries

Use these sources to keep welfare claims disciplined. The paper's core result
is positive: it maps mechanisms and selection scores before making normative
claims.

- Khosrowi and Beck, "Like It or Not: Recommender Systems Lack a Coherent
  Normative Foundation," preprint/forthcoming FAccT 2026.
  https://philsci-archive.pitt.edu/29368/
- "Endogenous preferences: a challenge to constitutional political economy."
  https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10602-023-09417-w
- von Weizsaecker, "Freedom, Wealth and Adaptive Preferences."
  https://www.econ.mpg.de/80411/CCvW-Freedom_-Wealth-and-Adaptive-Preferences.pdf

## Governance, Enforcement, And Regulation

These sources support the government channel. Government should be modeled as
an endogenous collective feedback mechanism with state capacity, capture, and
enforcement limits, not as an outside welfare planner.

- Becker, "Crime and Punishment: An Economic Approach," Journal of Political
  Economy, 1968.
  https://www.nber.org/chapters/c3625
- Bureau of Justice Statistics data portal, for enforcement, courts,
  corrections, victimization, and justice-system data.
  https://bjs.ojp.gov/data

  Management Science, 1971.
